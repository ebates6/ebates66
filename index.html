About me

I am currently a Dealer Credit Analyst for Ford Motor Credit Company. In my 18 years with FMCC, I've held a number of positions and been exposed to nearly every facet of the Automotive Finance industry. During my professional career, I developed a niche for implementing the latest technology to streamline the way FMCC does business. For example, while working as a Dealer Service Analyst, I created a SharePoint Team site to house sales and marketing communications; a Customer Service Department Training site to organize training documents and links to various training FAQs and videos; and a Status Compliance Peer-to-Peer (B2B) site to allow outside vendors to access related material without VPN access. I also pursued my personal interest in computers by completing the Comp TIA A+ certification along with numerous online courses.

After years of answering tech related questions; developing and managing various websites; and spending hours diagnosing and repairing personal computers, I decided to start my own personal computer repair company. I started out as a basic computer repair service. Since Upstate Computer Repair's inception 5 years ago, I have expanded the services to small business software solutions, website design and remote IT support.

Why Software Engineering Interests Me?

I want to become a software engineer because I want to create customized web-based solutions that are designed around the end-user's experience. I see software as a powerful way to display content; share ideas; and increase productivity; however, in my experience with existing software, I've found my self limited to generic templates and predesigned content structure that don't meet more specific needs. As a software engineer, I could create my own templates and customize the way content is displayed based on the end-user experience and deliver products that are easy to learn; fun to use; and meet the customers needs completely.

I also want to merge my experience as an employee of FMCC; my experience as a small business owner; and my newly obtained software development skills to create new ways to work smarter; not harder. I think that my unique perspective will empower me to create innovative software solutions that are easier to deploy and maintain in a small business environment.
